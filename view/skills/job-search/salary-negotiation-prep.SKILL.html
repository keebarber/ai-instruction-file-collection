Salary Negotiation Prep

When to Use This Skill

Use when the user wants to: negotiate a job offer or salary; research market rates; create a counter-offer strategy; understand total compensation; mentions "salary negotiation", "negotiate offer", "counter offer", "compensation", "how much should I ask for".

The Negotiation Mindset

  1. Negotiation is expected — companies budget for it.
  2. 84% of employers expect candidates to negotiate.
  3. Not negotiating leaves $500K-$1M on the table over a career.
  4. The goal is win-win, not adversarial.

What you're really negotiating: base salary, signing bonus, annual bonus/commission, equity (options, RSUs), benefits (401k match, insurance), perks (vacation, remote, professional development), start date, title.

Research Phase

Step 1: Determine Market Rate

Sources: Levels.fyi (best for tech), Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary, Blind, PayScale, Salary.com, H1B salary data. Build a range: Low (25th), Target (50th), High (75th), Stretch (90th percentile).

Step 2: Know Your Value

Increases worth: years of relevant experience, specialized/rare skills, track record of results, in-demand certifications, competing offers, employee referral, market demand. May limit: entry level/career change, less experience, skill gaps, location arbitrage.

Step 3: Calculate Total Compensation

Total Comp = Base + Bonus + Equity + Benefits

Base $150,000 + Target Bonus 15% ($22,500) + RSU 4-yr $200K ($50K/yr)
+ 401k Match 4% ($6,000) + Benefits ~$15,000 = $243,500 total annual comp

Equity terms: RSUs (real shares, taxed at vest); Options (right to buy at strike); Vesting (typically 4-year, 1-year cliff); Refresh grants (annual).

Negotiation Strategy

Best time: after you have a written offer, before you sign. Timeline: verbal offer → ask for written → ask for time to review → research 24-48h → counter → discussion → final agreement in writing.

The Counter-Offer Framework

  1. Express enthusiasm 2. Reinforce your value 3. Make specific ask 4. Provide justification 5. Open discussion

Counter-Offer Email Template

Subject: [Your Name] - Offer Discussion

Hi [Recruiter/Hiring Manager],

Thank you so much for the offer to join [Company] as [Title]. I'm very excited about the opportunity to [specific thing]. After speaking with the team and learning more about [something specific], I'm confident this is the right fit.

I've reviewed the offer and wanted to discuss compensation. Based on my research of the market and my [X years of experience / specific skill / competing offer], I was hoping we could discuss a base salary of $[Your Ask] rather than $[Their Offer]. [Optional justification.]

I'm flexible and open to discussing other elements of the package as well. Would you have time for a quick call?

Thank you again — I'm looking forward to finding a package that works for both of us.

Best, [Your Name]

Counter-Offer Call Script

"Hi [Name], thanks for making time. I'm really excited about this — [specific reason]. I've reviewed the offer and want to discuss compensation. Based on my market research and [X years / key accomplishment / competing offer], I was hoping for a base closer to $[Amount]. Is there flexibility there?" [LISTEN] If pushed back: "I understand there are constraints. I'm flexible — could we look at signing bonus, equity, or [other element] to bridge the gap?"

Common Scenarios

  • First offer is low: don't accept immediately; counter with research-backed number.
  • They ask your expectations first: "I'm flexible and focused on the right fit. What's the range you've budgeted?"
  • They won't budge on base: negotiate signing bonus, equity, earlier review, vacation, remote, dev budget, title, relocation, start date.
  • You have competing offers: only mention if true; frame as problem-solving, not a threat.
  • They ask current salary (illegal in many states): redirect to value + market rate.

Tactics

Do: always negotiate respectfully; get the offer in writing first; research thoroughly; be specific with numbers; express genuine enthusiasm; give them a way to say yes; consider total comp; be patient; get final agreement in writing. Don't: accept on the spot; give salary history (if not legally required); make ultimatums; lie about competing offers; be aggressive; negotiate just to negotiate; accept verbal promises; burn bridges.

Output Format

# SALARY NEGOTIATION STRATEGY
## Market Research Summary  (role, location, level; 25/50/75/90th percentile; sources)
## Their Offer              (base, bonus, equity, signing, total year 1)
## Your Counter             (component, ask, justification)
## Counter-Offer Script     (email or call, customized)
## If They Push Back        (Plan B elements + walk-away point)
## Key Talking Points
## Questions to Clarify      (equity vesting? bonus guaranteed? review cycle?)

Implementation Checklist

  1. Research market rate from 3+ sources
  2. Calculate total compensation (not just base)
  3. Identify your priorities
  4. Determine walk-away point
  5. Prepare counter-offer with justification
  6. Write or practice negotiation script
  7. Plan for pushback scenarios
  8. Get agreement in writing
  9. Review final offer letter carefully
  10. Sign
